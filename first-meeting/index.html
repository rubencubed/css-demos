Author Guidelines

Manuscripts submitted for consideration should present original research, critical analysis, or interdisciplinary scholarship relevant to the journal’s scope. Submissions must not exceed 7,000 words including references, notes, and appendices.

Authors are encouraged to include a concise abstract of no more than 150 words, followed by 4–6 keywords. Citations should conform to the latest edition of the Chicago Manual of Style. All submissions must be anonymized for peer review.

Papers should be submitted electronically in Microsoft Word format. Questions regarding manuscript preparation may be directed to Journals Support.

Publication Ethics

The journal adheres to internationally recognized standards concerning publication ethics, plagiarism prevention, and academic integrity. Submitted work must be original and may not be under review elsewhere.

Authors are responsible for obtaining permissions for copyrighted materials, images, and datasets used within their manuscripts. Cases of duplicate publication or fabricated data will result in immediate rejection.

Reviewers and editors are expected to disclose any conflicts of interest that could influence editorial decisions.

Editorial Board

The editorial board is composed of scholars and practitioners from a broad range of disciplines, institutions, and international research communities.

Board members oversee peer review, contribute to strategic direction, and advise on emerging areas of scholarship relevant to the journal’s mission.

A complete listing of editorial staff and advisory committee members is updated annually and published in the journal’s spring issue.

Book Review Policies

The journal welcomes reviews of recently published academic books that contribute substantially to debates within the humanities and social sciences.

Prospective reviewers should contact the book review editor before submitting a review essay. Unsolicited reviews may not be considered.

Standard reviews should range between 1,000 and 1,500 words and should critically engage the text’s methodology, contribution, and scholarly significance.

Abstracting & Indexing

The journal is indexed in several major academic databases, ensuring broad discoverability and accessibility for researchers, librarians, and institutions worldwide.

Articles are currently abstracted in Humanities International Complete, JSTOR, Scopus, and other discipline-specific indexing services.

Metadata updates and DOI registrations are processed upon publication to support citation tracking and long-term archival preservation.

Advertising Information

Limited advertising opportunities are available for academic presses, conferences, research institutes, and educational organizations whose missions align with the journal’s readership.

Advertising placements are accepted on a rolling basis for both print and digital editions. Rates and publication schedules are available upon request.

Subscription & Access

Individual and institutional subscriptions include online access to current and archived issues through the journal’s digital platform.

Discounted subscription rates are available for students, independent researchers, and nonprofit organizations. Institutional licenses may include campus-wide authentication and archival access.

For assistance with account access, renewals, or print delivery, please Email Journals.

History of Johns Hopkins University

1878 · The Press begins

Daniel Coit Gilman believed publishing was a noble duty of a great research university. In 1878, the Johns Hopkins Publication Agency began publishing the American Journal of Mathematics under J. J. Sylvester.

1881 · First book published

The Agency published its first book, Sidney Lanier: A Memorial Tribute, honoring the poet, musician, and early faculty member.

1882 · Historical and political studies

Herbert Baxter Adams established the groundbreaking series Johns Hopkins Studies in Historical and Political Science.

1891 · Johns Hopkins Press

The Publication Agency became the Johns Hopkins Press. Since 1972, it has been known as Johns Hopkins University Press, and more recently Hopkins Press for short.

1893 · Medical publishing begins

When the Johns Hopkins School of Medicine opened, medical publishing by the Press soon followed.

1977 · Hopkins Fulfillment Services

Hopkins Fulfillment Services began operations, offering warehousing, fulfillment, and other publishing services to publishing partners.

1995 · Project MUSE launches

Project MUSE launched with support from the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation and the National Endowment for the Humanities, in collaboration with the University’s Sheridan Libraries.

Today · A global university press

Hopkins Press remains one of the world’s largest university presses, publishing and distributing works that advance new knowledge worldwide.

CSS/HTML Used

  • contrast-color function: Automatically calculates the best color contrast (black or white) based on an input color, e,g, the background color. Guarantees at least WCAG AA compliance.
  • Relative Colors: Colors can be darkened, lightened, shifted around the color wheel automatically without the need for determining the fixed colors with a separate tool.

Automatic Light/Dark Mode

CSS/HTML Used

  • Color Scheme: Root level declaration that enables light-dark() and automatically switches undefined colors to canvas (background) and canvasText (color) on dark mode.
  • light-dark(): Allows two colors to be defined, one for each of light and dark modes. I recommend defining this once on a root-level variable when possible.
